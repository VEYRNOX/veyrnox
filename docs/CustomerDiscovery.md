# Veyrnox — Customer Discovery Script & Target List

> PURPOSE: validate (or kill) the core assumptions before building/raising further.
> This is the highest-value work for the seed goal — it produces the ONE thing
> Veyrnox lacks: evidence that real people want this and would pay.
>
> WHAT YOU'RE TESTING (the unvalidated hypotheses):
> 1. WEDGE — do "high-stakes / high-threat self-custodians" exist, in reach, with
>    a real pain the incumbents don't solve?
> 2. WILLINGNESS TO PAY — would they pay ~$12 / ~$40 / bespoke for security-privacy depth?
> 3. FEATURE TRUTH — which features actually matter (stealth? duress? inheritance?
>    multi-sig?) vs which are your assumptions?
>
> GOAL: ~10–15 conversations. You're LEARNING, not selling. If they say "no / I
> wouldn't pay / I don't have this problem" — that is a WIN (you learned it cheaply).

---

## THE GOLDEN RULES (so the data is real, not flattering)
1. **Don't pitch. Don't describe Veyrnox** until the very end. The moment you
   describe your product, they start being polite and the data dies.
2. **Ask about their PAST BEHAVIOUR, not hypotheticals.** "What did you do last time
   you…" beats "would you use a feature that…". People predict their future badly
   and flatter you; their past is real.
3. **Shut up and listen.** Ask, then be quiet. Let silences sit.
4. **Dig on emotion/pain.** When they sound frustrated or worried, ask "tell me more
   about that" — pain is where willingness-to-pay lives.
5. **Never ask "would you pay?" as yes/no.** Ask what they pay NOW for related things,
   and watch reactions to real numbers.

---

## SCREENING — is this person in the target audience?
You want people who SELF-CUSTODY meaningful crypto and/or have elevated risk. Quick filter:
- Do they hold crypto in self-custody (their own keys), not just on an exchange?
- Is the amount meaningful TO THEM (enough that losing it would hurt)?
- Bonus: any elevated-threat angle (public profile, high net worth, sensitive work,
  adversarial jurisdiction, prior theft/scam experience)?
If they only use an exchange / hold trivial amounts / have no security concern → they're
not your audience; note it and move on (that itself is signal about market size).

## THE CONVERSATION (≈20–30 min)

### Part 1 — Their world (no product talk)
- "Walk me through how you hold your crypto today — across what wallets/exchanges/devices?"
- "How did you end up with that setup?"
- "What are you most worried about with it, if anything?"  ← listen for the real fear
- "Has anything ever gone wrong — a scare, a scam, a near-miss, lost access?"
  "What happened? What did you do?"  ← past behaviour = gold

### Part 2 — The pain, dug deep
- "When you think about your crypto being lost or taken — what's the scenario that
  actually worries you?" (theft? phishing? device seizure? coercion? death/inheritance?
  forgetting keys?)
- "What have you done to protect against that? What did that cost you (time/money/effort)?"
- "What's still unsolved — what do you wish existed?"  ← the gap = your opportunity (or not)
- IF they raise threat/coercion/privacy unprompted → dig hard, this is your wedge signal.
  IF they never do, even when prompted → that's important evidence the wedge may be narrow.

### Part 3 — Test the specific feature assumptions (still no pitch)
Frame as "some people worry about X — does that resonate, or not really?"
- "Some people worry someone could force them to unlock their wallet. Is that a real
  concern for you, or not something you think about?" (duress/stealth signal)
- "How do you think about what happens to your crypto if you die or lose access?"
  (inheritance/social-recovery signal)
- "Do you care whether your wallet sends your activity to third-party servers for
  scam-checking, or is that fine with you?" (privacy/no-telemetry signal)
- "If you were hiding some holdings, would you want that across Bitcoin/Solana too, or
  is one chain enough? Would you hide wallets you already use, or set up separate ones?"
  (the multi-chain + existing-vs-new stealth questions — let THEM answer the design)
- Note which of these light them up vs get a shrug. The shrugs are as valuable as the hits.

### Part 4 — Money (carefully, near the end)
- "Do you pay for any security/privacy tools today? (VPN, password manager, hardware
  wallet, etc.) Roughly what?"  ← anchors real willingness-to-pay
- "What would make a crypto wallet worth paying a monthly fee, if anything? Or would
  you never pay for a wallet?"  ← listen honestly; many will say 'never' — that's data
- ONLY NOW, if it fits: "I'm working on a self-custody wallet focused on exactly the
  protections you mentioned — [reflect their words]. If it did [their stated need],
  is that something you'd pay, say, ~$12/month for? ~$40 for the serious version?"
  Watch the reaction to the REAL numbers — flinch, shrug, or lean-in.

### Part 5 — Close + referral
- "Who else do you know who self-custodies serious crypto / cares about this? Could you
  introduce me?"  ← referrals are how you reach a niche you don't have access to
- "Can I come back to you when I have something to show?"  ← these become your waitlist/design partners

## WHO TO TALK TO (you said you don't have direct access — so manufacture it)
Start with reach you DO have, then expand by referral:
1. **Your security-consulting network** — you have credibility here. Anyone in/near
   crypto-holding, high-net-worth, or security-sensitive work. Your warmest door.
2. **Crypto communities for serious holders** — self-custody / Bitcoiner / security-focused
   forums, Discords, subreddits, local meetups. Post asking to learn, not to sell.
3. **High-threat-adjacent groups** — privacy advocates, journalists/activists orgs
   (handle with care + ethics), OpSec communities. (These validate the C-wedge but may
   not be the payers — note that distinction.)
4. **High-net-worth / wealth-management adjacency** — people who hold real value and
   would pay for protection. (Tests the higher-price tiers.)
5. **People who've been scammed/hacked** — they have the scar and the motivation; search
   communities where victims gather.

Aim: ~10–15 conversations across these. Track each (audience type, their real pain,
did the wedge resonate, would they pay / at what price, will they refer).

---

## WHAT YOU'RE LISTENING FOR (the decision)
**GREEN (wedge is real, raise is fundable):** people repeatedly raise the same acute pain
unprompted, have tried/paid to solve it, light up at your features, and don't flinch at the
prices. → You have evidence. Build the deck around their actual words.
**YELLOW:** pain is real but they won't pay / it's a nice-to-have / market feels thin.
→ Reconsider pricing, wedge, or whether it's venture-fundable vs a smaller business.
**RED (kill/pivot signal):** you can't find these people, or they shrug at the pain, or
"I'd never pay for a wallet" is near-universal. → The wedge isn't there as imagined; far
better to learn this now than after more building. Pivot the wedge or rethink the goal.

## AFTER THE CONVERSATIONS
- Write up what you heard (patterns, exact quotes, where you were wrong).
- THEN build the deck — its problem/wedge/traction slides come from real quotes + signal.
- The people who said "come back to me" = your waitlist / design partners = your traction slide.

> Reminder: the product is strong enough to demo. The build is NOT the bottleneck.
> THIS is. Evidence from real conversations is what converts "good plan" into "fundable."
